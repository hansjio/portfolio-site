FAQ

A few things people usually ask before they reach out.

Yes. I'm finishing my degree but actively looking for roles where I can contribute from day one — whether that's data science, software engineering, or something that sits between both. Remote-first is ideal, but I'm open to relocation for the right opportunity.

Mostly backend systems, database architecture, and data-driven web apps. I've built ERP and POS systems, government record management tools, and analytics dashboards. Comfortable jumping in mid-project or building from scratch.

I start by understanding the actual problem, not just the stated requirements. From there it's scoping, a quick prototype to validate assumptions, then iterative builds. I document as I go and care a lot about clean handoffs — code that someone else can read and maintain matters to me.

For data work, Python all the way — pandas, scikit-learn, HuggingFace. For web, it depends on the project. I reach for PHP/MySQL for straightforward systems and React/Node for anything that needs more scale. SQL-heavy backends are home turf regardless of what's on the frontend.

Remote is my default and where I work best, but I'm open to relocation for full-time roles — especially if there's a strong team or meaningful mission behind it. I'm based in Manila, UTC+8.

TsekTxt is my undergraduate thesis and a prototype I'm building alongside it. The research benchmarks three transformer models — RoBERTa-Tagalog, mBERT, and XLM-RoBERTa — on detecting election misinformation written in Taglish, then uses SHAP and Integrated Gradients to surface which linguistic features each model actually responds to. The prototype turns that into a working web app.

Open to it. If you're working on something in NLP, misinformation detection, or the intersection of language and civic issues, feel free to reach out. Research with a social or policy angle is the kind of thing I find genuinely interesting.

Depends heavily on scope, but most of the systems I've worked on run anywhere from two weeks to three months. I can give a much more accurate estimate after a short first conversation about what you're actually trying to build.
